HPC Tournament Methodology

The Hierarchical Predictive Coding (HPC) Tournament leverages a multi-LLM (GLLLM) pipeline to score scientific literature against a 36-factor predictive-coding glossary. This process involves:

  • Hierarchical ingestion of neurophysiological manuscripts.
  • Ontology-constrained scoring via specialized MLLM agents.
  • Consensus validation across diverse model architectures (Gemma, Qwen, etc.).
  • Visual comparison of Gene vs. Label ontology mappings.

These scatter plots represent the terminal alignment of the Council's reasoning, grounding high-level scientific hypotheses in literature-derived evidence.
